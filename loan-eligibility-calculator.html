📐 How Loan Eligibility is Calculated

Banks use the FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) method. Most lenders allow up to 50% of your net monthly income to go toward total EMIs (existing + new loan).

Max EMI Allowed = (Monthly Income − Existing Obligations) × 50%
Eligible Loan = Max EMI × [(1+r)^n − 1] / [r × (1+r)^n]

Where r = monthly interest rate, n = total months

💡 Tips to Increase Eligibility

Close existing loans or credit card dues before applying. A longer tenure reduces EMI and increases the loan you qualify for — but means more total interest. A credit score above 750 helps you negotiate better rates, which also boosts eligibility.

⚠️ This is an Estimate

Actual loan eligibility varies by lender, credit score, employment type (salaried vs self-employed), age, city, and the property/asset being financed. Use this tool for planning — consult your bank for an exact sanction letter.
