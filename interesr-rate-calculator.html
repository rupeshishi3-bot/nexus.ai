🔢 How the Rate is Found

There's no direct formula to extract interest rate from EMI — it requires numerical iteration. This tool uses the Newton-Raphson method, which converges to an accurate solution within 100 iterations (typically much fewer).

EMI formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / [(1+r)^n − 1]

Given EMI, P, and n → we solve for r (monthly rate) numerically
Annual Rate = r × 12 × 100

📢 When is This Useful?

When a lender or car dealer tells you "pay just ₹X per month" without disclosing the interest rate, this tool unmasks the true rate. Some "0% EMI" schemes from credit cards also have hidden processing fees — this tool can reveal the effective rate when you factor those in as the extra cost over the principal.

⚡ Typical Market Rates (India 2025)

Home loans: 8–10% · Car loans: 8.5–12% · Personal loans: 12–24% · Credit cards: 36–42% · Payday loans: 60%+. Compare your calculated rate to these benchmarks to assess whether your loan is fairly priced.
