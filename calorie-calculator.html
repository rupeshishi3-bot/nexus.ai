🧬 How Calories Are Calculated

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, the most validated BMR formula for the general population. Your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the energy your body burns at complete rest. Multiplying by an activity factor gives your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure).

Male BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5
Female BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor

🥗 Macronutrient Split

For a balanced diet, a general guideline is 40% carbohydrates, 30% protein, and 30% fat. However, your ideal macro split may vary based on specific fitness goals — athletes may benefit from higher protein (up to 35%), while endurance sports may require more carbohydrate intake.

⚠️ Important Note

These estimates are based on population averages. Individual metabolism can vary by ±200–400 kcal/day due to genetics, muscle mass, hormones, and gut microbiome. If you've been tracking for 2–3 weeks with no change, adjust by 100–150 kcal up or down.
