📐 How Inflation is Calculated

Inflation reduces the real value of money over time. ₹1 lakh today will not buy the same goods 10 years from now.

Future Cost = Present Value × (1 + Inflation Rate)^Years
Real Purchasing Power = Present Value ÷ (1 + Inflation Rate)^Years

At 6% inflation, ₹1,00,000 today = ₹1,79,085 needed after 10 years

🇮🇳 India's Inflation Context

India's average CPI inflation has been around 5–7% annually over the past decade. The RBI targets 4% (±2%). This means your savings must earn at least 6–7% just to stay even. Equity mutual funds, which have returned 12–15% CAGR historically, are a popular inflation-beating tool.

💡 Rule of 70

A quick mental shortcut: divide 70 by the inflation rate to find how many years it takes for prices to double. At 7% inflation, prices double in 70÷7 = 10 years. At 6% it's about 11.7 years.
