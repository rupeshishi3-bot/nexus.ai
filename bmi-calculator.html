📐 How BMI is Calculated

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in metres. For example, if you weigh 70 kg and are 1.75 m tall, your BMI is 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9.

Formula: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)
Imperial: BMI = 703 × Weight (lbs) ÷ Height² (in²)

⚠️ Important Limitations

BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic one. It does not measure body fat directly and may misclassify athletes (who have high muscle mass) as overweight, or older adults with low muscle mass as normal. Always consult a healthcare professional for a full health assessment.

💡 Improving Your BMI

A combination of balanced nutrition, regular physical activity (150 minutes of moderate activity per week per WHO guidelines), adequate sleep (7–9 hours), and stress management are the most effective evidence-based approaches. Small, sustainable changes outperform drastic short-term diets.
