💧 How Water Needs Are Calculated

The base formula uses 33ml per kg of body weight, then adjusts for activity and climate. For example, a 70 kg adult with moderate activity needs approximately 2.3–2.8 litres per day.

Base: 33 ml × body weight (kg)
Activity add-on: +350–900 ml based on exercise intensity
Climate add-on: +200–800 ml based on heat and humidity

🌿 Signs of Dehydration

Early signs include dark urine (aim for pale straw colour), thirst, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating. Even mild dehydration of 1–2% body weight can reduce cognitive performance by up to 10% and physical performance by 20%.

☕ Does Tea & Coffee Count?

Yes — moderate tea and coffee do contribute to hydration. Despite being mild diuretics, the net fluid gain is positive. However, plain water remains the ideal source. Fruits and vegetables also contribute significantly — a diet rich in them can provide 20–30% of your daily fluid needs.
